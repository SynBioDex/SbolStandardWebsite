## Join the SBOL Community

SBOL is maintained and shaped by an open community of synthetic biologists, computational scientists, and industry professionals. There are many ways to get involved — from joining a conversation to actively contributing to the standard.

---

## Attend Workshops & Meetings

### SBOL Community Meetings
The SBOL community meets semi-annually online to discuss updates to the standard, review proposals, and coordinate tool development.

[View past and upcoming meetings →](/community-meetings/)

### HARMONY
A biannual workshop for harmonizing biological modeling and design standards, co-organized with the SBML and SBGN communities.

{{< cta cta_text="COMBINE Network" cta_link="https://co.mbine.org" cta_new_tab="true" >}}

### COMBINE
The Computational Modeling in Biology Network — a coalition of standards efforts for biological network models, co-organizer of HARMONY.

{{< cta cta_text="co.mbine.org" cta_link="https://co.mbine.org" cta_new_tab="true" >}}

### IWBDA
The International Workshop on Bio-Design Automation brings together researchers from computational and experimental synthetic biology.

{{< cta cta_text="iwbda.org" cta_link="https://www.iwbda.org" cta_new_tab="true" >}}

## Contribute to the Standard

### Submit an Enhancement Proposal (SEP)
The SBOL Evolutionary Proposal process is how the community extends and refines the standard. Anyone can submit a proposal.
